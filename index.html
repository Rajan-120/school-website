These pictures of Cicada were taken in our vicinity when they were perching on a bottle brush tree... One genus, the periodical cicadas spend most of their lives as underground nymphs, emerging only after 13 or 17 years.At least 3000 cicada species are distributed worldwide with the majority being in the tropics. Crickets make sound by rubbing their wings together, and cicadas have a special organ called a tymbal that produces sound. The tymbal contains a series of ribs that buckle one after the other when the cicada flexes its muscles.

“Living in harmony with Nature – not against it”

Using cloth bags instead of polythene:

We always carry cloth bags to bring grocery & vegetables from the market. To encourage their use, we have freely distributed hundreds of such bags on various occasions including my birthday.

Growing more trees:

In the small space on the front and back of our house we’ve grown many trees and plants like – Mango, Custard Apple, Neem, Parrot tree, Camel’s foot tree, Banana, Aloe Vera, Roses, Jasmine and many more. We also have many earthen pots in which we’ve grown beautiful all-season plants like – Adenium, dieffenbachia, Coleus, Kalanchoe, Ferns, Calathea, Caladium and many more.

Developing a nursery:

We utilize discarded packaging bags to grow stems. When they develop roots, we gift them to friends. Thus, we avoid the purchase of plastic gift items and non-biodegradable packaging.


Eco-friendly & handmade gifts: My mother stitched zipper pouches made of cotton cloth and I gave them to my teachers and classmates as a token of gratitude toward them.

Composting:

The discarded peels, stems and leaves from fruits & vegetables are put into a compost bin where earthworms decompose the organic matter. And we use the compost as a manure for plants.

Harmony with creatures:

For more than a decade, our letter-box has been the nesting place for sparrows. As there are plenty of plants around, Mynah, Purple-sunbird, Magpie-Robin, Tailor bird, sparrows and many others throng throughout the year, feeding on nectar, insects and berries. As a compliment, we enjoy their song and dance.
